Methodology

ARES Football Market separates on-field football quality from football asset value.

Two Scoring Systems

ARES Score measures on-field football quality. Market Score measures football asset value. A great player can have a lower Market Score because of age, durability, contract status, role risk, or market demand. A younger player can carry a higher Market Score than his current ARES Score because of upside, position scarcity, transfer interest, or contract optionality.

Launch Scope

Senior players and top development players can be ranked when the data quality supports it. Youth academy, loan, free agent, and thin-data players can remain watchlist-only until confidence improves.

Formulas

Football ARES Score = 0.30 * Performance + 0.20 * Efficiency + 0.15 * Role / Usage + 0.15 * Opponent Adjustment + 0.10 * Volume + 0.05 * Durability + 0.05 * Trend

Football Market Score = 0.25 * ARES Score + 0.20 * Age / Upside + 0.20 * Position Value + 0.15 * League Tier + 0.10 * Market Signal + 0.05 * Movement Value + 0.05 * Durability

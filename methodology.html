How ARES Score and Market Score Work

ARES Football Market separates on-field football quality from football asset value.

Methodology Status

The scoring framework is defined. The current player layer uses real Wikidata identities and licensed Wikimedia Commons photos, while ARES scores and stat fields remain beta estimates until approved live match, club, transfer, and availability data are connected.

What ARES Score Measures

ARES Score measures on-field football quality through performance, efficiency, role and usage, opponent adjustment, volume, durability, and trend.

What Market Score Measures

Market Score measures football asset value through ARES quality, age and upside, position value, league tier, market signal, movement value, and durability.

ARES Score Formula

Football ARES Score =\n0.30 * Performance\n+ 0.20 * Efficiency\n+ 0.15 * Role / Usage\n+ 0.15 * Opponent Adjustment\n+ 0.10 * Volume\n+ 0.05 * Durability\n+ 0.05 * Trend

Market Score Formula

Football Market Score =\n0.25 * ARES Score\n+ 0.20 * Age / Upside\n+ 0.20 * Position Value\n+ 0.15 * League Tier\n+ 0.10 * Market Signal\n+ 0.05 * Movement Value\n+ 0.05 * Durability

Example Market Score Calculation

A 21-year-old wide forward with ARES 86.7, strong age upside, scarce role, stable minutes, and rising movement signal can carry a Market Score above 92 even before becoming the top ARES player.

High ARES, Lower Market Example

A 29-year-old goalkeeper can have strong current ARES quality but a lower Market Score because age curve, contract horizon, and position liquidity reduce long-term asset value.

Young Player, High Market Example

A 19-year-old rotation striker can have a lower current ARES Score but a high Market Score if minutes are rising, role scarcity is strong, and the age curve suggests upside.

Position-Specific Components

Forward Module

Non-penalty goals, xG, shots, shot quality, touches in box, pressing actions, dribbles, off-ball runs, durability, and league adjustment.

Midfielder Module

Progressive passes, carries, chance creation, ball retention, defensive actions, press resistance, duels, minutes load, and league adjustment.

Defender Module

Tackles, interceptions, aerial duels, clearances, ball progression, error rate, defensive line role, availability, and league adjustment.

Goalkeeper Module

Save percentage, goals prevented, cross claims, distribution, sweeper actions, error rate, minutes, and league adjustment.

League Strength Adjustment

League strength is designed to prevent equal raw production from being treated the same across unequal competitions. Live outputs require connected league and player data.

Age Curve Logic

Age curve logic rewards upside windows, prime-year stability, and role durability while flagging aging, injury, and role-decline risk.

Transfer Signal Logic

Transfer signal logic tracks movement type, sending club, receiving club, league change, role opportunity, scarcity, and market impact.

What ARES Does Not Claim

ARES Score is not a transfer fee.
ARES Score is not a salary estimate.
ARES Score is not a fantasy rank.
ARES Score is not a scouting report by itself.
Market Score is not the same as market price.
Market Score is not a guaranteed sale value.
Market Score is not a betting line.
Market Score is not an official club valuation.

Source Policy

ARES does not claim to be official league data, does not replace scouting, does not claim to know assignments perfectly, and does not use restricted commercial scraping.

Image Policy

Player images must come from provider-supplied URLs, properly licensed Wikimedia Commons pages, TheSportsDB artwork, or the ARES fallback avatar. Do not scrape Transfermarkt, Google Images, club websites, agency previews, or social media.

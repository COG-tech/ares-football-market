Methodology

ARES Football Market separates on-field football quality from football asset value.

Two Scoring Systems

ARES Score measures on-field football quality. Market Score measures football asset value. A great player can have a lower Market Score because of age, durability, contract status, role risk, or market demand. A younger player can carry a higher Market Score than his current ARES Score because of upside, position scarcity, transfer interest, or contract optionality.

What ARES Score Measures

  • Performance
  • Efficiency
  • Role and usage
  • Opponent adjustment
  • Volume
  • Durability
  • Trend

What Market Score Measures

  • ARES quality
  • Age and upside
  • Position value
  • League tier
  • Market signal
  • Movement value
  • Durability

What Market Score Is Not

  • A transfer fee
  • A salary estimate
  • A contract value
  • A betting projection
  • A fantasy football rank
  • A scouting report by itself

Position Modules

Forward Module

Non-penalty goals, xG, shots, shot quality, touches in box, pressing actions, dribbles, off-ball runs, durability, and league adjustment.

Midfielder Module

Progressive passes, carries, chance creation, ball retention, defensive actions, press resistance, duels, minutes load, and league adjustment.

Defender Module

Tackles, interceptions, aerial duels, clearances, ball progression, error rate, defensive line role, availability, and league adjustment.

Goalkeeper Module

Save percentage, goals prevented, cross claims, distribution, sweeper actions, error rate, minutes, and league adjustment.

Formulas

Football ARES Score =\n0.30 * Performance\n+ 0.20 * Efficiency\n+ 0.15 * Role / Usage\n+ 0.15 * Opponent Adjustment\n+ 0.10 * Volume\n+ 0.05 * Durability\n+ 0.05 * Trend

Football Market Score =\n0.25 * ARES Score\n+ 0.20 * Age / Upside\n+ 0.20 * Position Value\n+ 0.15 * League Tier\n+ 0.10 * Market Signal\n+ 0.05 * Movement Value\n+ 0.05 * Durability

Data Safety

ARES does not claim to be official league data, does not replace scouting, does not claim to know assignments perfectly, and does not use restricted commercial scraping. Player images must come from provider-supplied URLs, properly licensed Wikimedia Commons pages, TheSportsDB artwork, or the ARES fallback avatar.
